Selling Gold in Connecticut

Whether it's scrap jewelry in Bridgeport, coins in New Haven, or bullion anywhere in Connecticut, a fair sale starts with one thing: knowing your gold's melt value before you accept an offer.

Calculate your gold's value

Know your value before you sell

Every gold offer in Connecticut is built from three numbers: the karat (purity), the weight, and the live gold spot price. A buyer then applies a payout percentage to cover refining and margin. If you know the melt value first, you can instantly tell a fair offer from a lowball one.

Where to sell gold in Connecticut

Connecticut sellers generally have five options, with the most choices in larger metros like Bridgeport, New Haven, and Stamford:

  • → Local jewelers — convenient and often fair for nicer pieces, though some only buy what they can resell.
  • → Dedicated gold buyers — focused on scrap and melt; compare their payout percentage carefully.
  • → Coin & bullion dealers — best for bullion and collectible coins, where premiums matter.
  • → Pawn shops — fast cash, but payout percentages are often lower; negotiate.
  • → Online mail-in buyers — competitive rates and insured shipping, with a return option if you decline the offer.

Whichever you choose, vet the buyer: look for clear reviews, a visible scale, and a written, itemized offer.

What determines your offer

  • → Karat / purity — 10K, 14K, 18K, 22K, or 24K; higher karats hold more gold per gram.
  • → Weight — measured in grams or pennyweight (dwt); confirm which unit the buyer uses.
  • → Payout percentage — the share of melt value offered, commonly 60–90% for scrap.
  • → Premiums — recognized bullion and collectible coins can sell above melt.

Taxes and rules in Connecticut

Sales-tax treatment of precious metals varies by state and by what you sell. Many states exempt investment-grade gold and silver bullion, while jewelry, scrap, and numismatic coins may be treated differently. Profits on a sale can also be subject to federal capital gains tax.

This page is general guidance, not tax advice. Confirm current Connecticut (CT) rules with the state's Department of Revenue or a qualified tax professional before a large transaction.

FAQ

Selling gold in Connecticut: questions

Where can I sell gold in Connecticut?

In Connecticut you can sell gold to local jewelers, dedicated gold and coin buyers, bullion dealers, and pawn shops — concentrated in larger metros like Bridgeport, New Haven, and Stamford — as well as reputable online mail-in buyers. Compare at least two or three offers, and always check the melt value first.

How much will I get for my gold in Connecticut?

Your offer depends on the gold's karat, weight, and the live spot price, minus the buyer's payout percentage. Most buyers pay roughly 60–90% of melt value for scrap. Calculate the melt value first so you can judge any Connecticut offer against it.

Is selling gold taxed in Connecticut?

Tax treatment varies by state and by what you sell. Many states exempt investment-grade bullion from sales tax, while jewelry, scrap, and numismatic coins can be treated differently, and capital gains may apply at the federal level. Confirm current Connecticut rules with the state's Department of Revenue or a tax professional before a large sale.

How do I avoid lowball gold offers in Connecticut?

Know your melt value before you walk in, ask for the price per gram or pennyweight and the payout percentage in writing, watch the scale, and get more than one quote. Buyers in Bridgeport and other Connecticut metros compete for business, so comparing offers pays off.
