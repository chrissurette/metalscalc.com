Calculator FAQ

Gold, silver, and spot price calculator basics.

How do I calculate scrap gold value?

Enter the gold weight, choose grams, dwt, or troy ounces, select the karat or custom purity, and calculate. MetalsCalc uses spot price, purity, and weight to estimate melt value.

Can I use this as a silver spot price calculator?

Yes. Select Silver, enter your weight, choose fine silver, sterling, coin silver, or custom purity, then calculate the estimated silver scrap value from the current spot price.

What is a spot price calculator?

A spot price calculator estimates metal value using the current market price per troy ounce. MetalsCalc supports gold, silver, and platinum spot calculations.

Can I edit the current spot price?

Yes. The spot price field fills automatically, but you can click it, type your own price, or use Refresh to return to the latest current spot price.

Does the calculator support 14K gold?

Yes. The gold calculator includes common karats like 24K, 22K, 18K, 14K, and 10K, plus a custom purity option for unusual alloys or refinery-specific percentages.

What does payout percentage mean?

Payout percentage is the percentage of estimated melt value offered to the seller. For example, an 80% payout means the offer is 80% of the calculated scrap value.
