10K vs 14K

10K vs 14K Gold: Which Is Better?

10K vs 14K gold compared: purity, durability, price, and melt value. See which karat is better for everyday jewelry and resale value.

Calculate the value

10K and 14K are the most common karats for affordable gold jewelry in the United States. 10K is 41.7% gold — the minimum that can legally be called gold — while 14K is 58.3% gold. The extra gold in 14K means more value and a warmer color; the extra alloy in 10K means more hardness and a lower price.

If budget and toughness lead your decision, 10K delivers. If color and resale value matter more, 14K is the step up that most buyers choose.

Which holds more value?

14K holds about 40% more melt value than 10K of the same weight, because it contains more gold (58.3% vs 41.7%). For resale, 14K is the stronger choice.

Which is more durable?

10K is technically the hardest because it has the most alloy, but 14K is also very durable and offers a better balance of toughness, color, and value for most people.

FAQ

10K vs 14K questions

Is 14K gold better than 10K?

14K has more gold (58.3% vs 41.7%), a richer color, and higher value. 10K is harder and cheaper. 14K is the more popular all-around choice.

Does 10K or 14K hold value better?

14K holds more melt value per gram because it contains more pure gold — roughly 40% more than 10K of the same weight.

Is 10K gold real gold?

Yes. 10K is 41.7% gold and is the minimum purity that can legally be sold as gold in the United States.

Which is better for sensitive skin?

14K contains less alloy than 10K, so it is slightly less likely to cause a skin reaction. Higher karats are generally gentler still.
