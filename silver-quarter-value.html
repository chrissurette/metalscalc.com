Silver Quarter

Silver Quarter Value

How much is a silver quarter worth? See the melt value of 90% silver quarters (pre-1965) at current and past spot prices, plus which quarters are silver.

Calculate coin value

U.S. quarters minted in 1964 and earlier are 90% silver. Each contains about 0.1808 troy ounces of pure silver, which is what gives common-date "junk silver" quarters their melt value. Quarters dated 1965 and later are copper-nickel clad and contain no silver.

The melt value of a 90% silver quarter is simply its silver content times the live silver spot price. The chart below shows that value across a range of spot prices.

FAQ

Silver Quarter questions

How much silver is in a quarter?

A 90% silver quarter (1964 and earlier) contains about 0.1808 troy ounces of pure silver. Its melt value is 0.1808 × the silver spot price.

Which quarters are silver?

Washington quarters dated 1932–1964, plus earlier Barber (1892–1916) and Standing Liberty (1916–1930) quarters, are 90% silver. 1965 and later quarters are copper-nickel clad with no silver.

Are any quarters after 1964 silver?

Circulating quarters from 1965 on are clad and contain no silver. Special 40% silver and .999 silver proof/collector quarters exist but are not found in pocket change.

How much is a silver quarter worth today?

At a silver spot of $58, a 90% silver quarter holds about $10.49 in melt value (0.1808 × $58). Rare dates and high grades can be worth more to collectors.
