About .900 coin silver

Coin silver is .900 fine — 90% pure silver. It is the alloy used in pre-1965 U.S. dimes, quarters, and half dollars ("junk silver"), as well as many older American flatware and hollowware pieces.

Enter the weight to estimate melt value at the live silver spot price. For bags of junk silver, weighing the lot is faster and often more accurate than counting face value.

FAQ

Coin Silver Calculator questions

How much is 90% silver worth per gram?

At a $58 silver spot, one gram of .900 coin silver holds about $1.68 in melt value (1 ÷ 31.1035 × 0.900 × $58).

How much silver is in a pre-1965 quarter?

A 90% silver quarter contains roughly 0.1808 troy ounces of pure silver. Weighing the coins and using the .900 setting gives a precise lot value.

What is "junk silver"?

Junk silver refers to common-date 90% silver U.S. coins with no collector premium — valued purely for their silver content.

Is coin silver the same as sterling?

No. Coin silver is .900 fine; sterling is .925. Use the matching purity so the melt estimate is accurate.
