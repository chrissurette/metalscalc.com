14K vs 18K

14K vs 18K Gold: Which Is Better?

14K vs 18K gold compared: purity, color, durability, price, and melt value. See which karat is better for rings, everyday wear, and resale.

Calculate the value

14K and 18K are the two most popular gold karats for fine jewelry. The difference comes down to how much pure gold each contains: 14K is 58.3% gold, while 18K is 75% gold. That single fact drives everything else — color, durability, price, and melt value.

Neither is simply "better." 14K trades some gold content for hardness and affordability, making it ideal for everyday rings. 18K offers a richer color and more gold value, which suits special pieces and buyers who want maximum purity in wearable jewelry.

Which holds more value?

Per gram, 18K is worth more because it contains more gold — about 29% more melt value than 14K of the same weight. If maximizing gold content matters to you, 18K wins on value alone.

Which is better for an engagement ring?

14K is the more practical choice for rings worn every day: it resists scratches and dents better and costs less. Choose 18K if you prefer a richer color and don't mind a little more care and cost.

FAQ

14K vs 18K questions

Is 18K gold better than 14K?

It depends on your priority. 18K has more gold (75% vs 58.3%), a richer color, and higher melt value, but it is softer and pricier. 14K is more durable and affordable for everyday wear.

Does 14K or 18K gold hold value better?

18K holds more melt value per gram because it contains more pure gold. Both track the gold spot price; 18K is simply a higher percentage of it.

Is 18K gold too soft for daily wear?

18K is softer than 14K and shows scratches and dents more readily. It is fine for rings with care, but 14K is the more durable everyday option.

How much more is 18K worth than 14K?
