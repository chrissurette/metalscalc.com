FAQ

Precious metals heatmap questions

What is a precious metals heatmap?

A heatmap is a color-coded grid showing how related markets — metals, miners, the dollar, and yields — are moving at a glance. Green typically means strength and red means weakness, helping you spot what is leading or lagging.

How do gold, silver, platinum, and palladium relate?

They often move together as a group on macro news, but each has its own drivers: gold is the safe haven, silver is part industrial, and platinum and palladium are tied heavily to autos and industry.

What do mining stocks tell you about metal prices?

Miners are leveraged to metal prices and sometimes move ahead of them. Broad strength or weakness in gold and silver miners can confirm or question a move in the underlying metals.
