About 14K gold

14-karat gold is the most common gold purity in U.S. jewelry. It is 14 parts gold out of 24, which works out to roughly 58.3% pure gold by weight. The rest is alloy metals like copper, silver, and zinc that add strength and color.

Because 14K is only about 58.3% gold, its melt value is a little over half of what the same weight of pure 24K gold would be worth. This calculator does the troy-ounce conversion and purity math for you using the live gold spot price.

Worked example at an illustrative gold spot of $4,000.00 per troy ounce (use the live price above for current figures):

10 g = 0.3215 troy ozx58.3% purityx$4,000.00 spot=$749.75 melt value

How to calculate 14K gold value

  1. Weigh the item. Use a digital gram or pennyweight scale. Heavier, solid pieces give the most reliable estimate.
  2. Select the metal and purity. Pick gold, silver, or platinum, then choose the karat or fineness. Use Custom to enter an exact percentage.
  3. Confirm the live spot price. The calculator fills in the current spot price automatically. Edit it if you use a different quote.
  4. Calculate and adjust payout. Press Calculate to see the melt value, then move the payout slider to model a buying offer.
FAQ

14K Gold Calculator questions

How much is 14K gold worth per gram?

Per-gram value depends on the live spot price. At a gold spot of $4,000 per troy ounce, one gram of 14K gold holds about $74.98 in melt value (1 gram ÷ 31.1035 × 0.583 × $4,000). Use the calculator above with the current spot price for an exact figure.

Is 14K gold 58.3% or 58.5% pure?

14K is exactly 14/24 = 58.33% gold. Some refiners round to 58.5%. The calculator defaults to 58.3% for 14K, and you can switch to Custom purity to enter your refiner's exact percentage.

Why is my 14K offer lower than the melt value?

Buyers pay a payout percentage of melt value to cover refining, risk, and margin. Use the payout slider to model an offer — for example, 80% of melt.

Does the calculator work for 14K white and rose gold?

Yes. White, yellow, and rose 14K gold are all about 58.3% pure gold — only the alloy metals differ. Use the same 14K setting for all three colors.
