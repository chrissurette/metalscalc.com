About this hub

Silver value comes down to weight, fineness, and the live silver spot price. The common grades are .999 fine (bullion), .925 sterling (jewelry and flatware), and .900 coin silver (older U.S. coins). Pick the matching grade and the math is the same for all of them.

Use the calculators below for an instant melt estimate, then explore the gold-silver ratio and silver analysis to understand where the market sits before you buy or sell.

FAQ

Silver questions

How do I calculate the value of silver?

Convert the weight to troy ounces (grams ÷ 31.1035), multiply by the fineness (e.g. 0.925 for sterling), then multiply by the live silver spot price.

What is the difference between sterling, fine, and coin silver?

Fine silver is .999 pure (bullion), sterling is .925 (jewelry and flatware), and coin silver is .900 (older U.S. coins). Use the matching grade for an accurate estimate.

How much is sterling silver worth per gram?

At a $58 silver spot, one gram of .925 sterling holds about $1.72 in melt value. Use the live ticker price for an exact figure.
