About gold by pennyweight (dwt)

Pennyweight (dwt) is the traditional unit U.S. jewelers and gold buyers use for weighing jewelry. There are 20 pennyweights in a troy ounce, so one dwt is exactly 1/20th of a troy ounce, or about 1.555 grams.

This calculator lets you weigh in pennyweight directly. Enter the dwt, choose the karat, and it applies the live gold spot price — no manual conversion needed.

FAQ

Gold Pennyweight (DWT) Calculator questions

How much is a pennyweight of gold worth?

One dwt of pure gold is worth spot ÷ 20. At a $4,000 spot that is about $200 per dwt for 24K, or about $116.60 per dwt for 14K (× 0.583).

How many grams is a pennyweight?

One pennyweight is about 1.55517 grams, and there are 20 dwt in a troy ounce.

Why do jewelers use pennyweight instead of grams?

It is a long-standing trade convention in North America. Pennyweight maps cleanly to the troy ounce (20 dwt = 1 ozt) used to quote gold.

Can I switch between dwt and grams?

Yes. The unit dropdown in the calculator converts between grams, pennyweight, and troy ounces instantly.
