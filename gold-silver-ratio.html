Gold/Silver Ratio

Relative value monitor.

The ratio shows how many ounces of silver equal one ounce of gold.

Desk note

A high ratio can suggest silver is cheap relative to gold, but timing still depends on momentum and premiums.

Interpretation

Common ratio zones

LowUnder 50

Silver has already outperformed. Gold may be relatively more attractive.

Middle50-80

Neutral zone where trend and premiums matter more than the ratio alone.

HighOver 80

Silver may be historically cheap relative to gold, but confirm with momentum.

Framework

How to use the ratio

Step 1Calculate

Divide gold spot price by silver spot price.

Step 2Compare

Check whether the ratio is high, low, or normal relative to recent history.

Step 3Confirm

Use price trend, dealer premiums, and liquidity before rotating metals.

Step 4Act carefully

The ratio can stay extreme for longer than expected.

Use Case

What the ratio does not tell you

Warning

Relative value is not a timing signal by itself.

The ratio helps compare gold and silver, but it does not know your purchase premium, taxes, shipping, liquidity, or storage constraints.

  • Use the ratio as a filter, not a command.
  • Silver can look cheap and still fall.
  • Premiums can make physical silver less attractive than spot implies.
