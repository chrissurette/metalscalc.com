About gold bracelet

Gold bracelets — tennis, bangle, link, and charm styles — vary widely in weight, which is the biggest factor in their melt value along with karat and the live gold spot price. A light bangle may weigh under 10 grams, while a solid link bracelet can exceed 30 grams.

Weigh the bracelet, choose the karat stamped on the clasp or tag, and the calculator estimates its gold value. Charms or stones that are not gold should be excluded from the metal weight where possible.

Worked example at an illustrative gold spot of $4,000.00 per troy ounce (use the live price above for current figures):

How to calculate gold bracelet value

  1. Weigh the item. Use a digital gram or pennyweight scale. Heavier, solid pieces give the most reliable estimate.
  2. Select the metal and purity. Pick gold, silver, or platinum, then choose the karat or fineness. Use Custom to enter an exact percentage.
  3. Confirm the live spot price. The calculator fills in the current spot price automatically. Edit it if you use a different quote.
  4. Calculate and adjust payout. Press Calculate to see the melt value, then move the payout slider to model a buying offer.
FAQ

Gold Bracelet Calculator questions

How much is a 14K gold bracelet worth?

A 12-gram 14K bracelet holds about $899.77 in melt value at a $4,000 spot (12 ÷ 31.1035 × 0.583 × $4,000). Heavier or higher-karat bracelets are worth more.

Are gold bangle bracelets solid or hollow?

Both exist. Hollow bangles weigh much less than solid ones of the same size, so they hold less gold. The calculator uses actual weight, which reflects this difference.

Do charms and stones count toward value?

Only the gold content counts toward melt value. Non-gold charms, beads, and gemstones should be excluded from the weight for an accurate estimate.

What karat are most gold bracelets?

Most are 10K, 14K, or 18K. Check the clasp or a small tag for the karat stamp; higher karats hold proportionally more gold value.
