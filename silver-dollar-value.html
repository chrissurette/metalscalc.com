Silver Dollar

Silver Dollar Value

How much is a silver dollar worth? Melt value of 90% Morgan and Peace silver dollars across spot prices, plus Eisenhower dollar silver content.

Calculate coin value

Morgan (1878–1921) and Peace (1921–1935) silver dollars are 90% silver and contain about 0.7734 troy ounces of pure silver each — the most silver of any common U.S. circulating coin. That gives even common-date silver dollars a substantial melt value.

Note that most Eisenhower dollars (1971–1978) are copper-nickel clad; only specially marketed collector versions are 40% silver. The chart below covers the 90% Morgan and Peace dollars.

FAQ

Silver Dollar questions

How much silver is in a silver dollar?

A 90% Morgan or Peace silver dollar contains about 0.7734 troy ounces of pure silver. Its melt value is 0.7734 × the silver spot price.

Are Eisenhower dollars silver?

Most Eisenhower dollars (1971–78) are copper-nickel clad with no silver. Only specially sold collector versions are 40% silver (about 0.3161 troy ounces).

What is the difference between Morgan and Peace dollars?

Both are 90% silver with the same silver content. Morgan dollars (1878–1921) and Peace dollars (1921–35) differ only in design; melt value is identical.

How much is a silver dollar worth?

At a $58 silver spot, a 90% silver dollar holds about $44.86 in melt value. Many Morgan and Peace dollars carry collector premiums well above melt.
