Gold Guide

Gold marks and purity explained.

Learn what common gold stamps mean, how karat converts to purity, and how to estimate melt value before buying or selling jewelry.

Quick rule

Karat divided by 24 gives the approximate gold purity. 14K is about 58.3% gold, while 18K is 75% gold.

Step 1

Read the mark, but do not stop there

Gold jewelry is often stamped with marks like 10K, 14K, 18K, 22K, 24K, 417, 585, 750, 916, or 999. These marks are a starting point. They tell you what the item is supposed to be, but wear, repairs, plating, counterfeit marks, and mixed parts can change the real value.

Step 2

Understand 14K vs 18K gold

14K

14K gold

14K gold is about 58.3% gold and 41.7% other metals. It is common because it balances gold content, durability, and price. For melt value, only the gold portion counts.

18K

18K gold

18K gold is 75% gold and 25% other metals. It usually has a richer gold color and higher melt value per gram than 14K, but may be softer depending on the alloy.

Step 3

Convert the mark into melt value

To estimate melt value, weigh the item, choose the matching purity in the calculator, confirm the current gold spot price, and calculate. If the item has stones, steel springs, non-gold clasps, or weighted parts, subtract those before relying on the number.

Step 4

Know when to test

Test when the stamp is missing, damaged, suspicious, or inconsistent with the item. Acid testing, electronic testing, and XRF testing provide different levels of confidence. For higher-value items, professional testing is worth the extra certainty.
