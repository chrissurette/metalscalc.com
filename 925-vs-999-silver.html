925 vs 999

925 vs 999 Silver: Sterling vs Fine

925 sterling vs 999 fine silver compared: purity, durability, tarnish, use, and value. See which silver is better for jewelry vs bullion.

Calculate the value

The numbers 925 and 999 describe silver purity. 925 (sterling) is 92.5% silver with 7.5% copper for strength; 999 (fine) is 99.9% pure silver. Sterling is the standard for jewelry and flatware because it is durable; fine silver is used for bullion because it is nearly pure.

For wearable pieces, sterling wins on durability. For stacking value and bullion, fine silver wins on purity. Both are easy to value from the silver spot price.

Which holds more value?

999 fine silver holds slightly more value per gram because it is nearly pure (99.9% vs 92.5%). The difference is about 8%, smaller than the karat gaps in gold.

Which is better for jewelry?

925 sterling is better for jewelry and flatware because the copper makes it durable. Pure 999 silver is too soft for pieces that get handled or worn daily.

FAQ

925 vs 999 questions

Is 999 silver better than 925?

999 is purer (99.9% vs 92.5%) and slightly more valuable, but softer. 925 sterling is more durable, which is why it is used for jewelry and flatware.

Does 925 silver tarnish more than 999?

Yes. The copper in sterling reacts with air and moisture, so 925 tarnishes more readily than nearly-pure 999 fine silver.

Is 925 or 999 silver worth more?

999 holds about 8% more silver value per gram than 925. Both track the silver spot price; 999 is simply a higher percentage of it.

Which silver is best for stacking?

999 fine silver bullion is the standard for stacking and investment because of its purity and recognized bullion forms.
