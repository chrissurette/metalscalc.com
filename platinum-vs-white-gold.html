Platinum vs White Gold

Platinum vs White Gold: Which Is Better?

Platinum vs white gold compared: color, durability, maintenance, hypoallergenic properties, price, and value. See which is better for rings.

Calculate the value

Platinum and white gold look similar but are very different metals. Platinum (usually 95% pure) is naturally white, dense, and durable. White gold is yellow gold (commonly 14K or 18K) alloyed with white metals and plated with rhodium to look bright white — that plating wears and needs occasional renewal.

Platinum costs more upfront and is heavier and hypoallergenic; white gold is lighter and more affordable but needs replating over time. The right choice depends on budget, skin sensitivity, and how much maintenance you want.

Which is better for an engagement ring?

Both are excellent. Choose platinum for a naturally white, hypoallergenic, low-color-maintenance ring, and accept the higher price. Choose white gold for a lighter, more affordable ring, and plan for periodic rhodium replating.

Which is better value?

White gold usually costs less upfront. Platinum costs more but never needs replating and is denser. Over many years the maintenance gap narrows the difference.

FAQ

Platinum vs White Gold questions

Is platinum better than white gold?

Platinum is naturally white, hypoallergenic, and needs no replating, but costs more. White gold is more affordable and lighter, but needs rhodium replating to stay bright white.

Does white gold need to be replated?

Yes. White gold is rhodium-plated to look bright white, and that plating gradually wears, typically needing renewal every one to three years.

Is platinum or white gold more expensive?

Platinum is usually more expensive due to its purity, density, and the amount of metal used, though prices vary with the platinum and gold spot markets.

Which is better for sensitive skin?

Platinum is hypoallergenic and the better choice for sensitive skin. White gold alloys, and sometimes the nickel in older pieces, can cause reactions.
