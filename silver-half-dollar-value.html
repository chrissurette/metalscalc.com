Silver Half Dollar

Silver Half Dollar Value

How much is a silver half dollar worth? Melt value of 90% (pre-1965) and 40% (1965–1970) Kennedy half dollars across spot prices, plus which are silver.

Calculate coin value

Half dollars come in two silver compositions. Coins dated 1964 and earlier — Kennedy (1964), Franklin, and Walking Liberty — are 90% silver and hold about 0.3617 troy ounces of silver. Kennedy halves dated 1965–1970 are 40% silver and hold about 0.1479 troy ounces. Halves from 1971 on (circulating) are clad with no silver.

FAQ

Silver Half Dollar questions

Are Kennedy half dollars silver?

1964 Kennedy halves are 90% silver. 1965–1970 Kennedy halves are 40% silver. 1971 and later circulating Kennedy halves are copper-nickel clad with no silver.

How much silver is in a half dollar?

A 90% silver half dollar holds about 0.3617 troy ounces of silver; a 40% (1965–70) Kennedy holds about 0.1479 troy ounces.

Which half dollars are 90% silver?

Walking Liberty (1916–47), Franklin (1948–63), and 1964 Kennedy half dollars are all 90% silver.

How much is a silver half dollar worth?

At a $58 silver spot, a 90% half holds about $20.98 and a 40% Kennedy about $8.58 in melt value. Collectible dates and grades can be worth more.
