18K vs 24K

18K vs 24K Gold: Which Is Better?

18K vs 24K gold compared: purity, durability, color, price, and melt value. See why 24K is purer but 18K is more practical for jewelry.

Calculate the value

24K is pure gold — 99.9% fine — while 18K is 75% gold mixed with alloys for strength. 24K has the deepest color and the highest value per gram, but it is soft enough to bend and scratch easily, which is why it is used mostly for bullion rather than everyday jewelry.

18K is the practical compromise: nearly all the richness of pure gold, but durable enough to wear. The right pick depends on whether you want a wearable piece (18K) or maximum gold content (24K).

Which holds more value?

24K holds the most melt value because it is pure gold — about 33% more than 18K of the same weight. For investment, 24K bullion is the standard.

Which is better for jewelry?

18K is far more practical for jewelry. Pure 24K is too soft for rings and bracelets that get daily wear, so most fine jewelry tops out around 18K.

FAQ

18K vs 24K questions

Is 24K gold better than 18K?

24K is purer (99.9% vs 75%) and worth more per gram, but it is very soft. 18K is better for wearable jewelry; 24K is better for bullion and investment.

Why isn't 24K gold used in jewelry?

Pure 24K gold is soft and bends or scratches easily, so jewelry usually uses 14K–18K alloys for durability.

How much more is 24K worth than 18K?

For the same weight, 24K holds about 33% more melt value than 18K (99.9% ÷ 75% ≈ 1.33).

Is 24K gold a good investment?

24K bullion tracks the gold spot price closely and is the standard form for investment-grade gold. Jewelry premiums make 18K less ideal purely as an investment.
