About gold necklace

A gold necklace's value comes from its karat, its weight, and the live gold spot price. Pendant necklaces combine a chain with a pendant, which may be a different karat or contain stones — so it helps to weigh and assess each part.

Enter the necklace weight, choose the karat, and the calculator estimates the gold melt value. For pendants with gemstones, exclude the stones from the metal weight where possible.

How to calculate gold necklace value

  1. Weigh the item. Use a digital gram or pennyweight scale. Heavier, solid pieces give the most reliable estimate.
  2. Select the metal and purity. Pick gold, silver, or platinum, then choose the karat or fineness. Use Custom to enter an exact percentage.
  3. Confirm the live spot price. The calculator fills in the current spot price automatically. Edit it if you use a different quote.
  4. Calculate and adjust payout. Press Calculate to see the melt value, then move the payout slider to model a buying offer.
FAQ

Gold Necklace Calculator questions

How much is a gold necklace worth?

It depends on karat and weight. A 20-gram 14K necklace holds about $1,499.62 in melt value at a $4,000 spot. Use the calculator with your exact weight and karat for a precise estimate.

Do gold necklaces with pendants weigh more?

Yes, the pendant adds weight. If the pendant is a different karat or holds stones, assess it separately for the most accurate estimate.

Is melt value the same as resale value?

Melt value is the metal floor. Designer or antique necklaces can sell above melt, while a buyer typically pays a payout percentage of melt for scrap.

How do I know my necklace is real gold?

Look for a karat stamp (10K–24K or 417–999). Unstamped or magnetic pieces should be tested before relying on a melt estimate.
